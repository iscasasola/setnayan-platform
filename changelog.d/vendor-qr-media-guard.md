## 2026-07-03 · feat(vendors): QR-in-media integrity guard — vendor-funnel QR codes embedded in website photos/videos are invalid

Owner-locked 2026-07-03: the QR generators (Shortlist/invite QR → `/vendor-invite/<slug>`, Locked QR → `/vendor/lock/<token>`) are the ONLY free customer-import channel and exist for in-person clients — a vendor may not embed their QR in media on their public website (`/v/[slug]`) to funnel marketplace visitors around the inquiry path.

- **Reject-at-save (authoritative, images):** server actions that persist vendor-website media (`saveVendorProfile` portfolio+logo diff-scan, `updateVendorProfileField` logo, `createVendorService`/`updateVendorService` showcase photos, `commitVendorService` cover photo) fetch the R2 bytes, decode any QR (sharp → raw RGBA → jsQR, both existing deps), and refuse the save when the payload targets a funnel path — directly or after server-side redirect resolution (`lib/vendor-qr-media-guard.ts`; closes the URL-shortener loophole; SSRF-checked per hop via `hostResolvesPublic` exported from `lib/safe-image-fetch.ts`). Fail-OPEN on scanner trouble — never blocks an honest save.
- **Client fast feedback + video coverage:** `<FileUpload qrGuard>` (new boolean prop) lazily runs `lib/vendor-qr-guard-client.ts` — canvas+jsQR decode of picked images and ~8 sampled frames of the ≤30s showcase clip, with verdicts (incl. shortener resolution) from new `POST /api/vendor/qr-guard`. Wired on portfolio, logo, service cover, showcase photos + video. The client layer is the ONLY pre-save video check (no server-side frame extraction / FFmpeg exists) — the report path + admin sweep backstop it.
- **Retro-scan → admin queue:** migration `20270504200000_vendor_qr_media_flags.sql` (admin-read/update RLS, service-role writes, dedup on `(vendor_profile_id, r2_ref)`); "Scan QR codes" + a review queue grafted onto `/admin/repost-watch` (flag-and-review only — never auto-deletes; reports skipped videos rather than silently narrowing coverage).
- Scope guard: matches only VENDOR-FUNNEL payloads, never "any QR" — genuine wedding portfolio photos legitimately contain guest/table/event QRs (Papic).
