## 2026-07-08 · fix(plan3d): arrival chain completes under starved rAF — sit-clip completion is wall-clock-owned

Root cause of the live "Where am I seated?" hang (walk + chair-pull-back sit play visually, but the pill never flips to "You're at &lt;table&gt;"): the demo often runs in environments that starve `requestAnimationFrame` — the embedded dev-preview panel keeps the page at `visibilityState: 'hidden'` and delivers frames in on-demand bursts. The Walker's `raw` is wall-clock, so one delivered frame completes the walk instantly ("onComplete fires right after the walk starts" — the observed live artifact), but the SitController phase machine advanced **at most one phase per frame** (pull → step → tuck → ~12 settle frames), so after the choreography *looked* seated, `finish()` → `onSeated` → `onWalkComplete` still needed a dozen frames the starved stream never delivered.

- `kit/sit-controller.tsx`: phase hand-offs now CARRY the clock remainder (`advance(next, spentMs)`) and the frame loop resolves every phase that time already paid for in the same frame — one post-mount frame lands the whole overdue clip and fires `onSeated`. At a healthy 60 fps the carry is &lt;17 ms (choreography unchanged, just no dead frame per boundary). Settle consumes time accrued since its last iteration, so a resumed tab closes the 5 cm under-tuck in one hop. Forward and reverse clips both converted; reduced motion untouched (still snaps + completes without frames).
- `plan3d-scene.tsx` (Walker): the completion one-shot (`firedRef`) + gait re-arm moved from a passive `[walk]` effect into the frame loop, keyed to the walk OBJECT (merged with the existing `velWalkRef` identity check). The passive effect flushed after the layout-time `useFrame` closure swap, so a fire landing in that window got re-armed — `onComplete` could fire twice for one walk (the "fires multiple times" live observation), and a fresh walk could briefly inherit a stale fired=true.
- `tests/e2e/plan3d-arrival.spec.ts`: second test added — stubs rAF via init script, starves the whole walk, then pumps frames by hand; arrival must land within the pump budget. Verified RED on the pre-fix build with the exact same budget, GREEN after; the plain arrival test stays as-is. Both green 3× against `next dev` (StrictMode) + reproduced/validated end-to-end in the real embedded-preview environment.

No visual change to the sit choreography, roam, retarget-mid-walk, chair detach/restore, seated persistence, or chase-cam behavior.
