## 2026-07-05 · fix(admin/payments): stop approvePayment surfacing a 500 on success + on short payments

Two admin-facing robustness fixes to `approvePayment` (app/admin/payments/actions.ts), both confirmed live in prod on 2026-07-05 during Setnayan-AI activation verification:

- **Success-but-error:** a fully-successful approval (payment matched → order paid → SKU activated → receipt issued) could still show the admin the generic "Something on our end didn't work" 500, because the final broad `revalidatePath('/dashboard', 'layout')` re-renders couple pages and a render-time fault in one of them (e.g. the checklist page's revalidate-during-render, Sentry `JAVASCRIPT-NEXTJS-B`) propagated as the action's error — making admins re-click an already-done reconciliation (double-process risk). That revalidation is now deferred to `after()` (post-response), and both buyer notifications are now best-effort (try/catch) so a notification write can never fail the reconciliation. The admin's own `/admin/payments` + `/admin/payouts` views still revalidate synchronously.
- **Shortfall guard 500 → inline notice:** a short/partial payment (a normal business case) hit `throw new Error("Can't mark this order paid yet…")`, which rendered the generic 500 instead of the helpful "₱X short" message (Sentry `JAVASCRIPT-NEXTJS-Y`, fired for a real order). The guard now `redirect()`s back to the queue with an inline `?notice=` banner explaining the payment was matched but the order not promoted, and how to record it as partial. The shortfall math (base × 12% VAT) is unchanged — only how the outcome is surfaced. New `notice`/`noticeType` searchParams render a banner on the payments page.

SPEC IMPACT: None. Behavior-preserving except for how already-decided outcomes are surfaced to the admin (no 500 on success; inline notice on shortfall). No pricing, schema, or SKU changes.
