## 2026-07-21 · feat(vendor): per-event booth POSTER — storage, booked-vendor gate, and the 2:3 rule

Owner directive 2026-07-21: *"we want a place where they can upload their banner/design for that event, and their official logo will also show."* This lands the trust layer — storage, the write gate, the QR guard and the aspect rule. The vendor-facing upload UI and the 3D render follow in their own PRs.

**Two surfaces, deliberately kept apart.** The **logo** is global, from their account (`vendor_profiles.logo_url`), already rendered on the 3D booth's `BoothSign` since v8 lit up `tier`. The **poster** is per (vendor, event) — the design a booked vendor makes *for that wedding*.

**New `event_vendor_booth_posters`, not a column on `event_vendors`.** A vendor can hold several `event_vendors` rows for one event (one per booked service/category), but the poster is one artwork per vendor per event. A column would duplicate it N times and leave "which row wins?" undefined; `UNIQUE (event_id, vendor_profile_id)` states the real cardinality. `poster_ref` holds the RAW `r2://bucket/key` exactly like `logo_url` — scene assembly resolves it, nothing is presigned at rest. RLS enabled at `CREATE TABLE` time; SELECT policy for the owning vendor (profile owner or team member) plus console admins, and **no write policy at all** — writes go exclusively through the RPC.

**`vendor_set_booth_poster(p_event_id, p_poster_ref)` is the only write path**, matching the cocktail surface's established contract (thin server action over a `SECURITY DEFINER` RPC that carries the entire gate). The gate mirrors `_cocktail_vendor_caps`' first two checks — caller owns or belongs to a `vendor_profile`, and that profile is BOOKED on the event (`contracted`/`deposit_paid`/`delivered`/`complete`) — but **deliberately not its cocktail-specific ones**. The poster belongs to the vendor's presence at the event, not to one room, so it must not depend on `cocktail_enabled` / `cocktail_vendor_edit` or the arrange/booth category split; a booked vendor whose booth sits in the reception can still dress it. A NULL/blank ref clears the poster, so set and clear share one entry point.

**QR-in-media guard applied** (owner-locked 2026-07-03). A poster is a *more* obvious vector than a logo: large vendor-composed artwork shown to every guest walking the room, so a funnel QR smuggled into it would be read by phones standing in the venue. Reuses `vendorQrGuardRejects` — the same authoritative server-side reject the website-media path uses, and fail-open on scanner trouble so an honest vendor is never blocked (the admin retro-scan remains the backstop).

**The 2:3 portrait rule (`lib/booth-poster.ts`, unit-tested).** Portrait reads correctly on a booth BACK WALL, which is narrow, and matches the pull-up-banner format PH vendors already design for. Fixing the aspect also fixes the render geometry — one plane mesh, no per-vendor aspect maths. Master `1024x1536` (power-of-two friendly for mipmaps), max 500 KB, JPG/PNG/WebP, with a `512x768` mobile derivative constant declared for the render PR: the guest walk is phone-first and a 1024x1536 texture is ~6 MB uncompressed in GPU memory, so ten branded booths would be ~60 MB on top of the room, crowd and figure atlases.

**Enforce, don't letterbox** — a rejected upload with a clear message beats a silently letterboxed poster the vendor never sees in context. A ±2% tolerance admits 1000x1500 and 1080x1620 while rejecting square, 4:3, landscape and 1:2. Every helper **fails open** on unreadable dimensions, honouring `FileUpload`'s `validateFile` contract that a content validator must never brick the upload path. 15 unit cases cover the exact master, common 2:3 exports, in-band drift, each rejected shape, the dimensions appearing in the error copy so a vendor can fix their artwork, and the fail-open paths.
